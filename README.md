The Labyrinth is not a place. It is a phenomenon. A primordial truth lurking beneath the fragile veneer of our physical world. It is older than time, more ancient than memory, and utterly indifferent to the lives it ensnares. To understand the Labyrinth is to lose yourself in it—but let this knowledge guide you, for it may be the only light in an eternal dark.


---

The Void: The Source of All Things

Before the first spark of creation, there was the Void. It is the foundation of existence, the base element upon which all else is built. The Void is not nothingness; it is potentiality. Endless, chaotic, and deafening, it hums with a haunting melody that permeates everything. The whispers of the Void echo with truths beyond human comprehension, their meaning lost to mortal minds but their presence undeniable.

The Void is not meant to be perceived. It exists outside the boundaries of human experience, a cacophony of hums, whispers, and dripping water that overwhelms the senses. Here, light has no place, and vision fails. It is not dark—it is absence. Those who wander the Void must rely on touch, sound, and instinct to navigate its infinite, shifting passages.

In its instability, the Void births anomalies—fractures in reality known as tears. These tears are breaches into the Labyrinth, unstable rifts where the Void touches the lower layers of existence. They emit a low hum, higher and faster than the haunting melody of the maze itself, drawing the curious and the desperate like moths to a flame.


---

The Labyrinth: Layers Beneath Reality

The Labyrinth is the first layer beneath the physical world, an infinite maze that shifts and warps without reason. It is the graveyard of the lost, a place where nothing belongs, and nothing should be. Its structure is maddeningly fluid, yet its walls resonate with a slow, steady bass rhythm—a melody so haunting and consistent that it seeps into your thoughts. As your time here stretches, you begin to hear whispers, faint at first, but growing louder and more insistent as your sanity and strength dwindle.

The green timer, ticking down with every passing second, is your grip on sanity, your hunger gnawing at your resolve. The blue timer is your thirst, the unrelenting dryness of your throat that threatens to steal your strength. The Labyrinth will not kill you directly, but it will erode you. Its oppressive hum will remind you that escape is fleeting, that this place is eternal, and that you are an intruder.

The Influence of Tears

Tears do not belong in the Labyrinth, yet they puncture its walls, scattering fragments of reality within its confines. Food and water—the green and blue dots—fall through these rifts, offering a brief reprieve for the lost. But tears bring more than sustenance. From the Void, they summon the wanderers, and from the SubLabyrinth, they drag forth the followers, predators in their purest form. These beings do not belong here any more than you do, but the Labyrinth twists to accommodate their presence.


---

The SubLabyrinth: The Depths of Predation

Beneath the Labyrinth lies the SubLabyrinth, a realm of red-charcoal halls where the walls seem to bleed the essence of primal fear. This place is the cradle of the followers, beings born of the SubLabyrinth’s essence. They are the embodiment of the predator, stripped of all pretense and civilized restraint. They are faster than you, more determined than you, and they will not stop until you are consumed.

The SubLabyrinth is an unforgiving domain, suffocating and sparse. The food and water that fall through the tears are scarce here, and the relentless hum of the maze becomes an oppressive weight. To survive in this place is to be hunted every second.


---

The Wanderers and the Transitionary Realm

The Void’s instability spawns the wanderers, creatures as alien as the realm that creates them. They patrol the transitionary Void, a pitch-black realm that lies between the physical world and the Labyrinth. Wanderers are not predators—they are manifestations of chaos, aimlessly patrolling their cacophonic home, creating hazards for any who venture into the Void. Their paths are unpredictable, their whispers incomprehensible, and their presence overwhelming.

The transitionary Void, though part of the Labyrinth’s hierarchy, is unstable and ever-changing. It is here that tears are born, ruptures that breach both up and down, linking realms that should remain separate. The Void creates, and the Labyrinth suffers the consequences.


---

You Do Not Belong Here

The Labyrinth does not want you. It does not need you. It does not care for you. Yet here you are, a fragile mote of life trapped in a place older than time itself. You cannot comprehend the purpose of this place, and to try is to invite madness.

The whispers of the walls are not for you. The hum of the maze is not meant to comfort you. The food and water you find are fragments of a reality you no longer belong to. The tears may promise escape, but they are as likely to plunge you deeper into the nightmare.

If you are to survive, it will not be because the Labyrinth allows it. It will be because you have defied it. Keep moving. Listen to the hum. Feel the walls. And when the tear hums near you, run toward it.

This is not your home, and it never will be. Escape is not guaranteed, but it is your only hope.
